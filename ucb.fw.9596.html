United Colors of Benetton, Catalogue, Fall/Winter 1995-1996

 

 

 

This super rare catalogue features the work of Oliviero Toscani, the creative director of United Colors of Benetton. Toscani's vision for the brand, along with his provocative imagery, saw United Colors of Benetton garner both popularity and critical acclaim during the 1980s, up until his departure in 2000. This vision is best seen through Colors Magazine, and is evidence of Toscani's artistic influence. Working with Dutch graphic designer Tibor Kalman, in order to create 'a magazine about the rest of the world', Colors Magazine was used to create awareness of cultural indifference, and equality, whilst simultaneously promoting Benetton as a lifestyle brand. Toscani went on to photograph all of Benetton's campaigns, and catalogues, creating a style still synonymous with the brands image today.

This large format catalogue is beautifully printed on an uncoated stock, with full bleed images on each page. People of Beijing, China, are photographed wearing garments from Benetton's 1995-1996 Fall/Winter Collection. Fashion aside, these photographs offer an engaging look at life in Beijing, and the people that inhabit it.
