Bosozuko Masayuki Yoshinaga

 

 

 

Spread image sourced from http://alias.dj/#/thumbnails/archive-173/1

 

Bosozuko is the name given to a specific sub-culture of bikers within Japan. Taking inspiration, both stylistically, and formally, from the Hells Angels in America, Bosozuko define themselves as a ‘gang’. Operating mainly in Metropolitan areas of Japan, such as Tokyo and Kyoto, they have become a gateway for youth involvement within the Yakuza. Photographer Masayuki Yoshinaga, an ex-Bosozucko member himself, presents an uncompromising look into this sub-culture. Yoshinaga’s photographs show the groups escapades through the night, contrasted with studio portraits of members, both by themselves, and as a group. Brilliantly designed by Gareth Hague, and UK publishing house Trolley Books, Yoshinaga's photographs are presented in a concertina format, spanning over 24 meters; making for a wonderful collaboration between photography and print.

Yoshinaga is well known for documenting various sub-cultures in Japan, where he was born and raised. His reportage style of photography, paired with his interest in youth-culture, has seen his work feature in alternative fashion magazines of the 1990s, such as The Face, Relax and, Dazed and Confused. Gothic and Lolita is another of Masayuki Yoshinaga’s revered books, in which the photographer documents the subculture of gothic fashion adopted by Japanese youth. Yoshinaga continues to document, and live in Tokyo, Japan.
