Relax, Issue 75.5, Special Issue featuring New Waves by Takashi Homma, 2003

 

 

 

This special issue of Relax, a Japanese style and culture magazine, features the ongoing body of work 'New Waves' by Takashi Homma.

'New Waves' focuses on a single subject matter, photographed between the years 2000 to 2007. Homma's depiction of waves, and the ocean more broadly, encapsulates commonly held narratives of freedom, mindfulness, and isolation. The soft aesthetic of each image, paired with the force of the waves speaks to these said narratives. Homma's work is often closely related to alternative fashion media of the 1990's and early 2000's, with 'New Waves' giving a deeper insight into the photographers practice.

Published in 2003, this issue of Relax acts as a pre-cursor to 'New Waves' published by Parco View in 2007, in which Homma's full body of work is presented. Featuring over 80 images of Homma's photographs, each photograph is presented as a full bleed image, with no accompanying text.
