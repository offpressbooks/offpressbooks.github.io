i-D, Issue 186, 'The Skin and Soul Issue'

 

 

 

In this issue, cover star Heidi Klum photographed by Max Vadukul, with inside cover star Sharleen Spiteri photographed by Juergen Teller. "Size Does Matter After the rebirth of cool that was White On Blonde, Texas are finally ready to capitalise on their credibility. And for Sharleen Spiteri, this time it’s personal. Interview by Lena Corner, photographed by Juergen Teller. Free Love Artists have always been concerned with the body but the current crop seem nothing short of obsessed, displaying nude models in museums and even spreading their legs for porn mags. Stuart Shave takes a walk on the art worlds wild side. Why Men are Scared of You The first fashion story photographed by celebrated American conceptual artist Richard Prince, styling by Camilla Nickerson. The Great Big Tit Debate Photography by Duc Liao, styling bu Gianne Couji. Special Body Portfolio As the freakishly proportioned Barbie celebrates her fortieth birthday, women are still being forced to contain and civilise their unruly body’s, says Caryn Franklin. The heterosexual males panic at his own cultural redundancy is translating into problems of body image that are just like those suffered by women, counters Tony Marcus. Plus an international selection of photographers show that today’s search for beauty goes more than skin deep.”

Other contributors include; Mark Anthony, Corrine Day, Craig McDean, Jean Baptiste Mondino, Venetia Scott, Wolfgang Tillmans, and many more.

i-D Magazine / May 1999 / 220 x 300 / $50
