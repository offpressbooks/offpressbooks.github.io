Raygun, Issue 42

 

 

 

Raygun was a monthly magazine founded by Marvin Scott Jarrett in Santa Monica, California, with its first issue published in 1992. The magazine served to inform its readers of the latest trends in music and style. Artistic Director David Carson’s subversive agenda was influential in setting the magazine apart from the neo-modernist approach to layouts and typography, commonly seen throughout 1990s. Jarrett’s time at popular American Rock ’n’ Roll magazine, ‘Creem’, informed Raygun’s focus on music; however, its content took a more contemporary approach, looking to give agency to lesser known, up and coming artists. Raygun also served as an important source for 90s fashion trends, with its grunge aesthetic being an ideal platform to showcase designers.

In this issue, James + D’arcy from the Smashing Pumpkins are interviewed about their record label Scratchie Records, giving insight into the many artist run labels of the time. Designer Susan Cianciolo, whose importance in the underground fashion scene of the early 90s in New York, styles a shoot with photography by Cheryl Dunn. Dunn’s work would later progress from stills into documentary film making, with a particular interest in sub-cultures and youth cultures she surrounded herself with.
