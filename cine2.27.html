Cine2, Issue 27, 'Night On Earth'

 

 

 

Published by Chanter on the release of the film into Japanese cinemas, this publication features interviews, articles, and imagery from Jim Jarmusch’s ‘Night On Earth’. The film depicts a series of conversations between drivers and their passenger, which takes the form of several vignettes. Jarmusch's ability to accurately, and interestingly portray real time, makes for a beautiful piece of cinema. With full colour stills applied to interesting layouts, and behind the scenes photography by Christine Parr and others, this publication serves as a important accompaniment to a wonderful film.

"To the audience of Chanter - There is no meaning to my film "Night On Earth", no moral, no lessons to be learned. Only the little details of daily life on our planet, in which, sometimes we can find some incidental but beautiful things. My best wishes to you all - sincerely, Jim Jarmusch"

All text in Japanese

Chanter / 1991 / 180 x 250 / $30
