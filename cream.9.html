Cream, Issue 9, Maison Martin Margiela, Edition 2008

 

 

 

Cream Magazine was founded by Takara Mak, an entrepreneur who is recognised for introducing international designers to Hong Kong. Before Cream, Mak had launched Milk magazine in 2001, which covered aspects of contemporary Japanese style. This gained wide spread success, which led to a collaboration between Mak, and Comme Des Garcons, in which they opened a 'Guerilla' concept store. The store, along with Mak's influence on Hong Kong's fashion scene, opened up conversations with other designers, leading to the inception of Cream in 2002. Produced by design studio Silly Thing, issues of Cream rarely adhere to the usual format, allowing the themes present to dictate its form.

This issue of Cream Magazine looks at the revered archives of fashion house Maison Martin Margiela, upon the houses 20th anniversary. This cohesive presentation of past seasons, as well as interviews with the house, is a rare look into the infamously elusive designer. Cream editor Irene Leung describes the issue as a 'visual diary of their past', which is supported through mainly image based content, going back as early as the houses first collection in 1988.
