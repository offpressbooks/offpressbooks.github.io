MARK, Issue 3, 'Freak'

 

 

 

Mark Magazine is a brainchild of notable Australian fashion figure, Mark Vassallo. Vassallo has worked as a stylist and art director for magazines such as; Vogue US, Vogue Australia, Australian Style, Oyster, and many more. Vassollo is now the creative director of Farfetch, whilst continuing to collaborate, and consult for designers.

Working with photographer Justin Smith, and Art Director Shane Sakkeus, Freak is the third issue of Mark Magazine, and compiles over 400 images, all shot over the course of a year, in Perth, Western Australia. Freak is a culmination of Australian fashion in the early 2000s, and lays claim to discovering iconic Australian super model Gemma Ward. Additional imagery by Dutch-Australian artist Richard Woldendorp encapsulates the beauty of the Australian landscape through his well known aerial photographs. These photographs portray an almost mystifying scene, marrying well with that of Vassallo, Smith, and Sakkeus’ vision for the magazine. Printed on newspaper stock, the full bleed images take on a soft, yet rich aesthetic quality, highlighting its exquisite production.

Freak is as much a magazine, as it is a work of art. Published in a small run of 800.

Mark Vassallo / 2005 / 210 x 270 / slight tear at backcover / P.O.A
